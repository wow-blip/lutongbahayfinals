Laing ‚Äì taro leaves in spicy, creamy coconut milk

Overview

Laing is a traditional dish from the Bicol region of the Philippines, famous for its intense spiciness, creamy texture, and rich flavor from taro leaves cooked in coconut milk.

Servings: 4-5

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gata (coconut milk, first extract)
  • 1/2 cup gata (coconut milk, second extract)
  • 1 pound taro leaves (dried or fresh)
  • 1 whole onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger (optional)
  • 1/4 cup bagoong alamang (fermented shrimp paste)
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 whole red pepper, chopped
  • 1/4 cup siling labuyo (bird's eye chili, chopped)
  • 1 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Bring the first extract of coconut milk (gata) to a boil in a pot.
  2. Remove the taro leaves and soak them in a light salt and water solution to remove bitterness.
  3. Add the remaining coconut milk (second extract) to the pot.
  4. Add onions, garlic, and ginger, and let the mixture simmer slowly.
  5. Add the bagoong alamang (shrimp paste) and black pepper, stirring well.
  6. Stir the taro leaves into the creamy mixture.
  7. Add the chopped chili peppers and salt.
  8. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce heat to simmer.
  9. Cover and cook until the taro leaves are soft and the sauce has thickened.
  10. Garnish with red chili and fried shallots before serving with rice.
