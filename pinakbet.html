Overview

Pinakbet is a traditional Filipino dish originating from the Ilocano region. The name "pinakbet" comes from the Ilocano word "pinakebbet," meaning "shrunk" or "shriveled," referring to how the vegetables cook down during the process. This hearty stew features a variety of vegetables cooked in a savory anchovy-based sauce, often with pork for added flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb pork belly, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp chili sauce
  • 1 small squash, cubed
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bitter gourd (ampalaya), sliced
  • 10 okra, halved
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 10 yard long beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 taro (gabi), cubed
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups water or stock

Instructions

  1. Cut the pork belly into 1/2 inch cubes. Season with salt and black pepper, and set aside.
  2. In a pot, saut√© the garlic, ginger, and onions until fragrant.
  3. Add the tomatoes and cook until softened.
  4. Add the chili sauce, oyster sauce, and fish sauce, stirring until the sauce thickens.
  5. Add water or stock and bring to a boil.
  6. Add the pork belly and hard vegetables (squash, eggplant, bitter gourd, okra, yard-long beans).
  7. Add the bell pepper and taro.
  8. Cover the pot and boil until the vegetables are tender.
  9. Stir regularly to ensure flavors are well-mixed.
  10. Once done, let it simmer until the sauce thickens.
